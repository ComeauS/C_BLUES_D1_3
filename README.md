# C_BLUES_D1_3
Dataset of the response of BCE organisms to multiple drivers


C-BLUES is funded by the European Union under Grant agreement ID 101137844.

Deliverable D1.3 provides a database compiling experimental information on the responses of key Blue Carbon Ecosystem (BCE) organisms to environmental stressors. The dataset integrates experimental data generated within the C-BLUES project with complementary information extracted from the scientific literature.
The experimental data originate from laboratory and mesocosm experiments conducted within Task 1.2, focusing on organism-level responses of key BCE primary producers, here seagrasses and macroalgae. These experiments investigate how physiological and metabolic processes including growth, photosynthetic performance, respiration, calcification, and decomposition respond to environmental drivers such as temperature, pH, light availability, nutrient concentrations, and salinity.
To complement these experimental datasets, a targeted literature review was conducted. The literature selection followed a two-step approach. First, studies investigating the effects of multiple environmental drivers on BCE organisms were selected and the main drivers identified. Second, quantitative data were extracted only from studies examining the combined effects of temperature and pH. These drivers were selected because they represent major climate-related stressors that can be reliably projected under future climate scenarios and are therefore particularly suitable for integration into predictive ecosystem models.
The resulting dataset provides harmonized information on BCE organism responses to environmental change and will serve as the basis for meta-analyses and modelling activities within the project. The compiled dataset is provided as an XLS spreadsheet accompanying this deliverable. A curated and stable version of the database will be deposited in an open-access repository (Zenodo) at a later stage of the project once remaining ongoing analyses are completed. Data describing ecosystem-level processes including community metabolism, sediment carbon storage, and greenhouse gas fluxes are being compiled separately and will be provided in Deliverable D1.4 next year.
